# Riff Standalone Character Sprint 01

## Sprint objective

Move Riff from “a strong DeskBuddy theme character” to a coherent standalone
character with protected canon, an independent visual identity, a repeatable
voice, and enough story machinery to support multiple formats.

## Definition of done

By the end of this sprint, another writer or artist should be able to create a
short Riff scene that feels recognizably correct without using the DeskBuddy
theme as the only reference.

## Workstreams

### 1. Canon foundation

- [x] Establish standalone character status
- [x] Lock immigrant-to-Canada premise
- [x] Preserve birthplace and arrival details as intentional unknowns
- [x] Define Bret Hart's childhood influence
- [x] Define the maple leaf as a personal symbol
- [x] Clarify Canadian-identity and Indigenous-history guardrails
- [x] Separate Riff canon from the DeskBuddy implementation

### 2. Origin story

- [x] Create “The Leaf Before the Flag” treatment
- [ ] Decide where young Riff first sees the maple leaf
- [ ] Decide whether Bret appears through live broadcasts, reruns, or recordings
- [ ] Choose Riff's approximate life stage during the Canadian recognition scene
- [ ] Write the first polished 800–1,200 word version

### 3. Independent visual development

- [ ] Produce a standalone model sheet without DeskBuddy UI framing
- [ ] Add the imperfect maple-leaf patch to the canonical outfit
- [ ] Preserve the star pin as the separate side-quest symbol
- [ ] Explore restrained pink-and-black “resolve” accents
- [ ] Create front, side, back, and three-quarter turnarounds
- [ ] Create expression keys: wonder, resolve, homesickness, suspicion, delight,
  embarrassment, exhaustion, and quiet belonging
- [ ] Test the silhouette in monochrome and at sticker size

### 4. Voice

- [x] Establish voice qualities and guardrails
- [ ] Build a reviewed bank of 40 lines across eight emotional categories
- [ ] Write one dialogue scene with a friend
- [ ] Write one silent scene where body language carries the story
- [ ] Identify three phrases Riff would never say

### 5. World and supporting cast

- [x] Define Toronto after dark as the primary story territory
- [x] Define the annotated night map
- [x] Establish repeatable story engines
- [ ] Define three relationship roles without naming characters yet:
  - the person who plans;
  - the person who stays home;
  - the person who knows the city's forgotten stories.
- [ ] Choose whether humans and anthropomorphic animals share the same world
- [ ] Establish how public or secret the rave/explorer community is

### 6. First format experiment

Create one small, complete Riff story in a format that tests both character and
world:

- recommended: an eight-panel vertical comic based on “The Leaf Before the Flag”;
- alternate: a 45–60 second dialogue-light animated short;
- alternate: a four-page illustrated storybook scene.

The experiment should contain:

- one funny observation;
- one quiet emotional beat;
- one act of care;
- one visual side quest;
- the maple leaf without turning the story into a civics lesson.

## Acceptance criteria

- Riff reads as a raccoon before wardrobe or colour is added.
- His immigrant identity affects his inner life without becoming his only story.
- Canadian identity is expressed through lived belonging, not stereotype.
- Bret Hart's influence is recognizable as values and visual echoes, not copied
  intellectual property.
- Riff can carry a quiet scene, not only a rave scene.
- The maple leaf, star pin, map, and satchel each have distinct meanings.
- At least five story premises can be generated without referencing coding or
  DeskBuddy.
- The character remains cute, but cuteness never removes agency or complexity.

## Deferred creator decisions

These should not be guessed during the sprint:

- country and community of birth;
- family structure;
- age at immigration;
- citizenship status;
- exact age;
- romantic orientation;
- whether Riff's name predates Canada;
- named supporting characters;
- the intended primary audience;
- the first commercial format.

## Recommended next decision

Choose the emotional temperature of Riff's immigration history:

1. **Warm but complicated:** loving earlier home, difficult transition, secure
   present.
2. **Restless:** multiple moves, fragmented idea of home, Canada is the first
   place he stays.
3. **Protective mystery:** Riff rarely discusses the circumstances, and the
   story reveals them slowly.

This choice will shape the origin story more than choosing a birthplace would.
